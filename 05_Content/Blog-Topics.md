# PropFlo Blog Topics

## Purpose

This document is the working blog topic backlog for PropFlo.

It expands the blog ideas in the SEO-Content-Plan into a fuller list with intent, audience, and priority.

Use this file to plan, brief, and track individual articles.

## 1. Main Content Principle

Each article must earn its place.

Every blog topic should support at least one of these goals:

- Explain the offer
- Build trust
- Support SEO
- Support paid media landing pages
- Answer seller questions
- Support partner referrals
- Reduce sales friction

Do not write articles that do none of these.

## 2. How To Use This List

For each topic, confirm:

- The main audience
- The search intent
- The primary keyword
- The funnel stage
- The priority

Then brief the article and record it in the Content-Calendar.

Compliance wording must be approved before publishing anything that describes the product, fees, timing, or qualification.

## 3. Funnel Stages

Use these three stages:

- Awareness: the reader is learning about the problem or topic.
- Consideration: the reader is comparing options and checking if they qualify.
- Decision: the reader is close to enquiring and needs reassurance.

## 7. Seasonal And Reactive Topics

Keep a short list of reactive topics for later, once the core content is live:

- Property market updates relevant to sellers
- Common transfer delays during busy periods
- End-of-year cash-flow pressure for sellers
- Moving season and seller costs

Only write these if they support the core offer and stay within approved wording.

## 8. Topic Rules

For every topic:

- Use plain language.
- Avoid hype and fear-based finance marketing.
- Avoid aggressive loan-style wording.
- Do not state fees, timing, or qualification rules that are not yet approved.
- Link softly to the Check if you qualify page.

## 9. Compliance Notes

Before publishing any article, PropFlo must approve:

- Product wording
- Qualification wording
- Fee wording
- Repayment wording
- Approval and payout timing
- Required disclaimers

Where a detail is not approved, describe the general idea and mark specifics as [TO CONFIRM].

## 10. Starting Recommendation

Write the Priority 1 topics first, after the FAQ and landing pages are live.

People need to trust the core offer before regular articles will convert.

Record every published article in the Content-Calendar and review which topics bring qualified enquiries.
