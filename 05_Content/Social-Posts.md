# PropFlo Social Posts

## Purpose

This document holds draft social media post ideas for PropFlo.

It covers organic support posts for the channels in the marketing strategy, mainly LinkedIn and Meta.

All posts here are drafts. None are approved for public use yet.

## 1. Main Social Principle

Social media is a support channel, not the primary lead channel.

Its job is to build trust, explain the offer, and support paid media and partner referrals.

Do not treat likes, reach, or impressions as final business results.

## 2. Compliance Status

Approval status:

Not yet approved.

Before any post goes live, PropFlo must confirm the product, claims, and disclaimer wording.

Do not use restricted claims such as guaranteed approval, instant cash, same-day payout, no checks, no documents, no risk, or no fees.

## 3. Tone And Style

Posts should be:

- Professional
- Calm
- Clear
- Reassuring
- Helpful

Posts should avoid:

- Hype
- Fear tactics
- Cash-pile imagery
- Countdown timers
- Desperate seller language
- Payday loan styling
- Guaranteed approval badges

Follow the Brand-Guidelines and Creative rules for all visuals.

## 4. Post Themes

Rotate between these themes so the feed is not repetitive:

1. Education: explain the transfer gap and how bridging finance works.
2. Trust: process clarity, security, professional handling.
3. Questions: answer one common seller question per post.
4. Partner: how estate agents and conveyancers can refer sellers.
5. Process: simple step-by-step of the enquiry journey.

## 10. Posting Notes

- Keep a simple content mix across the themes above.
- Do not over-post. Quality and clarity matter more than volume.
- Every post that mentions the product, fees, timing, or qualification needs approved wording first.
- Record posts and their performance so the team can see what supports real enquiries.

## 11. Compliance Notes

Before publishing any post, PropFlo must approve:

- Product wording
- Qualification wording
- Fee and repayment wording
- Approval and payout timing
- Required disclaimers
- Privacy wording

Mark any unconfirmed detail as [TO CONFIRM] and do not present it as fact.

## 12. Starting Recommendation

Start with a small number of education and trust posts once the offer and disclaimers are approved.

Use social to support paid media and partner outreach, not to replace them.
