# PropFlo Partner Sales Materials

## Purpose

This document defines the partner sales materials needed for estate agents, conveyancers, bond originators, and property professionals.

The goal is to help partners understand PropFlo clearly and refer suitable sellers without making risky or unapproved claims.

## 1. Main Principle

Partner materials must be simple, professional, and compliance-safe.

Partners should understand:

- Who PropFlo helps
- When a seller may need help
- What PropFlo does
- What partners may say
- What partners must not promise
- How to refer a seller
- What happens after referral

## 2. Primary Partner Audiences

### Estate Agents

Estate agents may identify sellers who are waiting for transfer and need access to funds.

### Conveyancers

Conveyancers may understand where a transaction is in the transfer process, but any referral process must respect professional boundaries.

### Bond Originators

Bond originators may work with clients who are buying, selling, or managing property finance timing issues.

### Property Developers and Investors

Developers and investors may be involved in multiple transactions where timing and cash flow matter.

## 3. Core Partner Message

Suggested wording:

PropFlo helps qualifying property sellers explore bridging finance linked to a property sale before transfer is complete.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo before use.

## 5. What Partners May Say

Suggested approved-style wording:

- PropFlo may be able to assist qualifying sellers who need access to funds before property transfer is complete.
- Assistance is subject to qualification, verification, approval, and PropFlo's terms and conditions.
- PropFlo can review your situation and explain whether you may qualify.
- I can introduce you to PropFlo if you would like to enquire.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo.

## 6. What Partners Must Not Say

Partners should not say:

- You are guaranteed to be approved.
- You will receive money today.
- There are no checks.
- There are no costs.
- The process is guaranteed.
- PropFlo will definitely help you.
- You do not need to read the terms.
- This is risk-free.
- This will not affect your transaction.

## 7. Estate Agent Referral Message

Suggested message:

Good day [Client Name],

You mentioned that you may need access to funds while waiting for your property transfer to complete.

PropFlo helps qualifying sellers explore bridging finance linked to a property sale before transfer is complete.

If you would like, I can introduce you to the PropFlo team so they can explain the process and check whether you may qualify.

Assistance is subject to qualification, verification, approval, and PropFlo's terms and conditions.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo.

## 8. Conveyancer Introduction Message

Suggested message:

We are contacting you regarding PropFlo, a service that assists qualifying property sellers with bridging finance linked to property sale proceeds before transfer is complete.

We would like to share information about the process, documentation requirements, and referral boundaries so that any communication remains clear and professional.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo.

## 9. Partner Email Template

Subject:

PropFlo referral information for property sellers

Please let us know if you would like us to send the partner information pack.

Kind regards,  
The PropFlo Team

## 11. Partner Process

Recommended process:

1. Partner identifies a possible seller need.
2. Partner gives the seller approved basic information.
3. Seller agrees to be introduced or contacts PropFlo directly.
4. PropFlo receives the enquiry.
5. PropFlo contacts the seller.
6. PropFlo checks basic qualification.
7. PropFlo updates the lead status.
8. Partner is updated only where appropriate and allowed.

## 12. Partner Tracking

Each partner referral should record:

- Partner name

- Partner type
- Lead name
- Date referred
- Lead source
- Lead status
- Qualification status
- Funded deal status

## 15. Starting Recommendation

Start with a small controlled partner group.

Do not launch a broad partner programme until the wording, process, tracking, and referral rules are tested.
