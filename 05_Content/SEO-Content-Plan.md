# PropFlo SEO Content Plan

## Purpose

This document defines the initial SEO and educational content plan for PropFlo.

The goal is to answer the questions property sellers and property professionals may search for before they trust or enquire about bridging finance.

## 1. Main SEO Principle

PropFlo content should not be written like generic finance marketing.

It should answer real questions clearly.

The content must help people understand:

- What bridging finance is
- Who it is for
- When it applies
- What documents are needed
- What the risks and conditions are
- How the process works
- What happens during property transfer

## 2. Primary SEO Audience

The primary SEO audience is the property seller.

This person may be searching because they have sold a property, are waiting for transfer, and need money before final proceeds are paid out.

## 3. Secondary SEO Audiences

Secondary audiences include:

- Estate agents
- Conveyancing attorneys
- Bond originators
- Property developers
- Property investors
- Family members helping a seller research options

## 8. Search Intent Map

### Informational Search Intent

People want to understand the topic.

Examples:

- what is property bridging finance
- how does bridging finance work
- how long does property transfer take
- what happens if transfer is delayed

Recommended content:

- Blog posts
- FAQ pages
- Explainer pages

### Commercial Search Intent

People are comparing or considering options.

Examples:

- property bridging finance South Africa
- bridging finance for property sellers
- advance on property sale proceeds
- finance before property transfer

Recommended content:

- Service landing page
- Qualification page
- Comparison-style content
- FAQ page

### Partner Search Intent

Professionals want to understand whether they can refer clients.

Examples:

- bridging finance for estate agents
- property seller finance referral
- conveyancer bridging finance
- finance solution for property sellers

Recommended content:

- Partner landing pages
- Partner FAQ
- Referral process page

## 9. Content Tone

Content should be:

- Clear
- Calm
- Professional
- Helpful
- Accurate
- Non-pushy
- Free of hype
- Easy for non-finance people to understand

Avoid:

- Guaranteed approval
- Instant cash
- Desperate seller language
- Fear tactics
- Legal certainty unless approved
- Financial advice unless approved
- Overpromising
