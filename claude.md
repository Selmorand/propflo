# PropFlo Marketing Project Instructions

## Project Purpose

This project is a complete working marketing system for PropFlo.

PropFlo operates in the property bridging finance space.

The project contains strategy, proposal planning, budgets, website planning, paid media, content, creative direction, tracking, CRM workflows, reporting, and monthly marketing management.

This is not primarily a software development project.

Treat this folder as a professional marketing agency workspace.

## Main Objective

Help Interon manage PropFlo marketing from proposal through implementation, launch, optimisation, reporting, and ongoing strategy.

The primary business goal is to build a measurable lead generation system that tracks performance from:

Traffic

to

Lead

to

Qualified Lead

to

Application

to

Approval

to

Funded Deal

The most important performance metric is cost per funded deal where the data is available.

## Working Rules

Before making changes, read the relevant existing files.

Do not create duplicate documents when an existing file can be updated.

Do not create unnecessary new folders or files.

Keep the current numbered folder structure.

Respect existing file names unless there is a strong reason to change them.

When updating strategy, make sure related files remain consistent.

For example, if the budget changes, check whether these files also need updating:

- 02_Proposal/Budget-Proposal.md

When the marketing strategy changes, check related files for contradictions.

## Compliance Rule

PropFlo is finance-related.

Do not invent or assume:

- Legal status
- Credit provider status
- Registration status
- Qualification rules
- Minimum or maximum funding amount
- Fees
- Interest
- Repayment terms
- Approval times
- Payout times
- Guarantees
- Disclaimers

Use these files as the main compliance controls:

- 00_Admin/Compliance-Questions.md
- 00_Admin/Approval-Records/Approved-Claims.md
- 00_Admin/Approval-Records/Restricted-Claims.md
- 00_Admin/Approval-Records/Approval-Log.md

If a claim is not approved, mark it for confirmation rather than presenting it as fact.

Do not use claims such as:

- Guaranteed approval
- Instant cash
- Same-day payout
- No checks
- No documents needed
- No risk
- No fees

unless they have been explicitly approved and recorded.

## Strategy Priorities

The current channel priority is:

1. Google Search Ads
2. Seller landing page and conversion funnel
3. Tracking and lead source attribution
4. SEO and educational content
5. Estate agent partner marketing
6. Conveyancer information and referral relationships
7. Meta retargeting
8. LinkedIn partner marketing
9. Email and WhatsApp follow-up
10. CRM and automation improvements

Do not recommend adding many new channels without a clear reason.

## Audience Priorities

Primary audience:

Property sellers

Secondary audiences:

- Estate agents
- Conveyancers
- Bond originators
- Property developers
- Property investors

Use:

- Simple and reassuring language for sellers
- Practical referral language for estate agents
- Formal and restrained language for conveyancers
- Commercial language for developers and investors

Do not use identical messaging for all audiences.

## Paid Media Rules

Start with controlled testing.

Do not recommend scaling ad spend until:

- Tracking works
- Landing pages work
- Lead handling works
- Lead quality is known
- Sales feedback is available

The first priority is proving cost per qualified lead and cost per funded deal.

Avoid vanity metrics as primary success measures.

Do not treat clicks, likes, impressions, or reach as final business results.

## Content Rules

Content should support at least one of these goals:

- Explain the offer
- Build trust
- Support SEO
- Support paid media
- Answer seller questions
- Support partner referrals
- Reduce sales friction
- Improve conversion

Use plain language.

Avoid hype.

Avoid fear-based finance marketing.

Avoid aggressive loan-style wording.

## Creative Rules

Visual direction should be:

- Professional
- Calm
- Trustworthy
- Property-focused
- Clear
- Minimal
- Process-oriented

Avoid:

- Cash piles
- Desperate people
- Urgent red warning graphics
- Luxury lifestyle promises
- Payday loan styling
- Guaranteed approval badges
- Countdown timers

Use:

- Timelines
- Checklists
- Property process diagrams
- Referral flows
- Simple icons
- Clear hierarchy

## Lead Management Rules

Use these core lead stages:

- New Lead
- Contact Attempted
- Contacted
- Not Qualified
- Qualified
- Documents Requested
- Documents Received
- Application Submitted
- Approved
- Declined
- Offer Sent
- Offer Accepted
- Funded
- Lost
- Duplicate
- Spam

Do not treat a form submission as a successful final conversion.

## Reporting Rules

Monthly reporting should explain:

- What happened
- What worked
- What did not work
- What changed
- What should happen next
- Budget recommendation
- Risks and blockers

Where possible, report:

- Total leads
- Qualified leads
- Applications
- Approvals
- Funded deals
- Cost per lead
- Cost per qualified lead
- Cost per funded deal

Always connect marketing data to sales outcomes where possible.

## Monthly Workflow

Weekly:

- Review paid campaigns
- Review search terms
- Review negative keywords
- Review lead quality
- Review response times
- Review landing page issues
- Update optimisation log

Monthly:

- Collect campaign data
- Collect lead and sales data
- Prepare monthly report
- Prepare next-month recommendations
- Recommend budget changes
- Update content priorities

Quarterly:

- Review positioning
- Review channel effectiveness
- Review partner programme
- Review SEO growth
- Review conversion bottlenecks
- Decide whether to scale, adjust, or restructure
