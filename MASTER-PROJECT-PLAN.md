## 2. Project Purpose

The purpose of this project is to build and run a controlled, measurable customer acquisition system for PropFlo bridging finance.

PropFlo helps qualifying property sellers explore funding linked to their property sale before transfer is complete. The marketing programme exists to generate and qualify seller enquiries, develop referral relationships with property professionals, and measure performance from first click through to funded deal.

This is not a social media management or "run some adverts" engagement. It is a structured programme covering:

- Customer acquisition — attracting qualifying sellers who are actively searching.
- Conversion — turning traffic into qualified enquiries through landing pages, forms, and clear messaging.
- Partner development — building estate agent, conveyancer, and other professional referral channels.
- Measurement — tracking the full journey and proving cost per qualified lead and cost per funded deal.

The most important business outcome is a measurable, repeatable acquisition process that can be improved and scaled responsibly, not vanity metrics such as impressions, likes, or clicks.

## 4. Project Scope

Scope is organised into workstreams. Each has an objective, deliverables, dependencies, responsible party, and approval requirement.

### WS1 — Discovery and Compliance
- Objective: Confirm product, legal, compliance, claims, and sales process before any public marketing.
- Deliverables: Completed discovery notes; completed compliance questions; approved claims list; restricted claims list; required disclaimers.
- Dependencies: PropFlo legal/compliance input; product and qualification detail.
- Responsible: Interon Strategy with PropFlo Compliance/Legal Adviser.
- Approval: PropFlo must approve all claims and disclaimers before use.

### WS2 — Strategy and Research
- Objective: Confirm audience, offer, competitor, keyword, and measurement baseline.
- Deliverables: Confirmed audience profiles; offer structure sign-off; keyword and negative-keyword lists; measurement baseline.
- Dependencies: WS1 product clarity.
- Responsible: Interon Strategy.
- Approval: PropFlo confirms audience and offer positioning.

### WS3 — Tracking and Measurement
- Objective: Measure the full journey from click to funded deal.
- Deliverables: Tracking plan; GA4/GTM configuration; Google Ads conversions; UTM structure; lead-source capture; KPI dashboard; reporting template.
- Dependencies: Website/landing pages (WS4); analytics and ad-platform access.
- Responsible: Interon Paid Media / Technical Support.
- Approval: Tracking must be tested and confirmed before launch.

### WS4 — Website and Landing Pages
- Objective: Convert traffic into qualified enquiries.
- Deliverables: Seller landing page; "Check if you qualify" form; thank-you page; FAQ page; then partner and "How it works" pages.
- Dependencies: Approved claims (WS1); site platform. **See Section 5 — this overlaps the low-code build's Phase 2. Commercial decision required.**
- Responsible: To be confirmed (Interon or the platform build team) — **decision required**.
- Approval: PropFlo must approve all page copy, forms, and disclaimers.

### WS5 — Paid Search (Google)
- Objective: Capture high-intent seller searches through controlled testing.
- Deliverables: Campaign structure; ad groups; approved ad copy; conversion actions; budget pacing.
- Dependencies: WS1 claims; WS3 tracking; WS4 landing pages.
- Responsible: Interon Paid Media.
- Approval: PropFlo approves ad copy; platform financial-services verification may be required.

### WS6 — Meta Retargeting
- Objective: Re-engage website visitors who did not enquire.
- Deliverables: Retargeting audiences; pixel/consent setup; approved creative.
- Dependencies: WS3 pixel; WS4 traffic; compliance approval.
- Responsible: Interon Paid Media.
- Approval: PropFlo approves wording; used only if compliant.

### WS7 — SEO and Content
- Objective: Build trust and organic visibility and support paid landing pages.
- Deliverables: SEO content plan; FAQ and educational articles; content calendar execution.
- Dependencies: WS1 approved wording.
- Responsible: Interon Content and SEO.
- Approval: PropFlo approves any content stating product, fee, timing, or qualification detail.

### WS8 — Partner Marketing (Estate Agents, Conveyancers)
- Objective: Establish high-trust referral channels.
- Deliverables: Partner pages; one-page explainer; referral process; partner email/WhatsApp templates.
- Dependencies: WS1 approved referral wording; PropFlo referral-fee position (legal check).
- Responsible: Interon Strategy / Content.
- Approval: PropFlo approves referral wording and any referral-fee arrangement.

### WS9 — LinkedIn B2B Marketing
- Objective: Build professional credibility and partner reach.
- Deliverables: Organic partner content; outreach structure for principals, conveyancers, bond originators.
- Dependencies: WS8 partner materials.
- Responsible: Interon Strategy / Content.
- Approval: PropFlo approves professional messaging.

### WS10 — Email and WhatsApp Follow-Up
- Objective: Support lead nurturing and fast response, subject to consent.
- Deliverables: Enquiry confirmation; document checklist; next-step sequences; approved WhatsApp scripts.
- Dependencies: WS1 consent/POPIA wording; PropFlo lead-response process.
- Responsible: Interon Content with PropFlo Sales.
- Approval: PropFlo approves scripts and consent wording.

### WS11 — CRM and Lead Management
- Objective: Ensure every lead is captured, staged, and followed consistently.
- Deliverables: Lead stages; required lead fields; follow-up workflow; sales handover.
- Dependencies: PropFlo lead owner and CRM/lead-tracker. **May overlap the low-code build's CRM/lead integration — decision required.**
- Responsible: PropFlo Sales with Interon Strategy.
- Approval: PropFlo confirms lead owner, response targets, and process.

### WS12 — Reporting and Optimisation
- Objective: Report performance and drive weekly/monthly improvement.
- Deliverables: Weekly optimisation log; monthly report; next-month recommendations.
- Dependencies: WS3 tracking; PropFlo sales-outcome feedback.
- Responsible: Interon Paid Media / Strategy.
- Approval: PropFlo reviews monthly and approves budget changes.

### WS13 — Project Management and Client Communication
- Objective: Keep the programme on schedule, on scope, and clearly communicated.
- Deliverables: Schedule; meeting notes; approvals log; change control; escalation.
- Dependencies: All workstreams.
- Responsible: Interon Project Lead with PropFlo Executive Sponsor.
- Approval: PropFlo approves scope changes in writing.

## 5. Out of Scope

The following are excluded from the marketing programme unless separately quoted and approved:

- Full website rebuild, full brand identity design, full CRM implementation.
- Legal, financial, credit, FICA, FSP/NCR regulatory advice or compliance approval.
- Translation, video production, photography, advanced marketing automation.
- Call-centre or sales-team management.
- Advertising spend (paid by PropFlo directly to platforms).
- Third-party software subscriptions and platform fees.

### Critical overlap with the platform build — Commercial decision required

PropFlo has explored a **low-code platform build** (the direction PropFlo has indicated it is taking) and an alternative Umbraco custom build. These were exploratory build-option estimates, not finalised proposals, but their scope materially overlaps this marketing programme:

- The low-code build's **Phase 2 ("Umbraco CMS marketing site and lead capture")** already includes the marketing website, responsive build, lead-capture forms, **Meta campaign landing pages, tracking pixels, and CRM lead integration**.
- The marketing budget separately prices a **landing-page set** and parts of its **setup fee** (funnel/landing-page planning, tracking structure, Meta setup, CRM workflow) for the same deliverables.

If both are charged as written, PropFlo would pay twice for the website, landing pages, tracking pixels, and CRM lead routing.

**Commercial decision required before proposal approval:** assign the website / landing pages / tracking pixels / CRM lead-routing deliverable to **one** source (recommended: the platform build), and re-scope the marketing programme to campaign, content, optimisation, and reporting work that sits on top of the built site. Until this is resolved, WS4 and parts of WS3/WS11 are marked overlapping.

## 12. Cost Control Method

- **Approved scope baseline.** Work is delivered against the signed scope only. Anything outside it follows change control (Section 16).
- **Change request process.** All additions are quoted for cost and time and approved in writing before work starts.
- **Separate media spend.** Ad spend is separate from Interon fees and paid by PropFlo to the platforms.
- **Third-party cost approval.** Subscriptions and platform fees are approved by PropFlo before commitment.
- **Monthly budget review.** Spend, pacing, and cost per qualified lead are reviewed monthly with a budget recommendation.
- **No scaling without evidence.** Budget increases only where lead quality and cost per qualified lead justify it.
- **Written approval for out-of-scope work.** No out-of-scope work proceeds without written PropFlo approval.

## 18. 90-Day Decision Framework

At the end of 90 days, management must be able to decide to **scale, maintain, restructure, pause a channel, change the offer, improve conversion, or expand partner marketing.**

Information required to make that decision:

- Cost per qualified lead by channel.
- Cost per funded deal, where data is available.
- Qualified lead volume and quality trend.
- Application, approval, and funded-deal counts.
- Which keywords and search terms produced qualified leads, and which wasted spend.
- Landing page and form conversion performance.
- Lead response time and sales feedback quality.
- Partner channel early signals.
- Tracking completeness and data reliability.

Decision logic (summary): scale channels with acceptable cost per qualified/funded deal; maintain channels that are learning; restructure or pause channels producing poor-quality or unprofitable leads; revisit the offer or conversion path if traffic is good but conversion is weak.
