# PropFlo Campaign Testing Plan

## Purpose

This document defines how PropFlo campaigns should be tested during the first 90 days.

The goal is to avoid wasting budget and to learn which channels, keywords, adverts, landing pages, and messages produce qualified leads.

## 1. Main Testing Principle

Do not test too many things at once.

Start with a controlled campaign, collect clean data, then expand.

The first goal is not maximum lead volume.

The first goal is to identify which traffic produces qualified leads and funded deals.

## 2. First Campaign To Test

Start with Google Search Ads for property sellers.

Reason:

People searching on Google are already showing intent.

They may already be looking for property bridging finance, seller finance, or funding before transfer.

## 3. Initial Campaign Structure

### Campaign 1: Property Seller Search

Ad groups:

1. Property Bridging Finance
2. Seller Finance
3. Transfer Finance
4. Sale Proceeds Advance

### Match Types

Use:

- Exact match
- Phrase match

Avoid broad match at the start.

## 4. Initial Budget Test

Suggested first month ad spend:

R15,000 to R25,000

Suggested daily budget:

R500 to R850 per day

Start at the lower end if tracking, landing pages, or lead handling are still being tested.

## 5. What To Test First

### Test 1: Keyword Intent

Find out which search terms produce real enquiries.

Track:

- Which keywords get clicks
- Which keywords generate leads
- Which keywords generate qualified leads
- Which keywords waste spend

### Test 2: Ad Message

Test two or three message angles:

1. Property sold but waiting for transfer
2. Access funds linked to your property sale
3. Check whether you may qualify

### Test 3: Landing Page

Start with one main seller landing page.

Improve it based on:

- Form completion rate
- Questions from leads
- Drop-off points
- Sales feedback
- Common objections

### Test 4: Lead Quality

Track whether leads are:

- Qualified
- Not qualified
- Duplicate
- Spam
- Not contactable
- Strong opportunities

## 6. Weekly Testing Routine

Every week, review:

- Campaign spend
- Search terms
- Cost per click
- Leads generated
- Cost per lead
- Lead quality
- Not-qualified reasons
- Landing page questions
- Form issues
- Sales feedback

## 7. What To Optimise Weekly

Optimise:

- Negative keywords
- Low-performing keywords
- Poor ad copy
- Weak landing page sections
- Form questions
- Budget allocation
- Location targeting
- Device performance
- Lead follow-up process

## 8. When To Pause Something

Pause a keyword, ad, or campaign if:

- It spends money but produces no leads
- It produces irrelevant leads
- It attracts people looking for personal loans
- It attracts people without property sale status
- It creates confusion
- It breaches approved wording or compliance limits

## 9. When To Increase Budget

Do not increase budget just because clicks are high.

Increase budget only when:

- Tracking is working
- Leads are being contacted quickly
- Leads are relevant
- Qualified lead rate is acceptable
- Cost per qualified lead is acceptable
- PropFlo can handle more enquiries
- There is evidence of applications or funded deals

## 10. Month 1 Testing Goal

Month 1 goal:

Prove whether Google Search Ads can generate relevant property seller enquiries.

Main questions:

1. Are people searching for this service?
2. Which keywords work?
3. What does a qualified lead cost?
4. Are leads being contacted fast enough?
5. Does the landing page explain the offer clearly?
6. What objections are appearing?

## 11. Month 2 Testing Goal

Month 2 goal:

Improve lead quality and add support channels.

Possible additions:

- Meta retargeting
- SEO article publishing
- Partner outreach
- LinkedIn partner posts
- Email follow-up
- WhatsApp scripts

Do not add all of these at once unless the team can manage them properly.

## 12. Month 3 Testing Goal

Month 3 goal:

Scale what works and reduce what does not.

Actions:

- Increase budget on proven keywords
- Pause poor keywords
- Improve landing pages
- Expand SEO content
- Build referral partner list
- Improve lead handling
- Review cost per funded deal if available

## 13. Testing Metrics

Track:

- Spend
- Clicks
- CTR
- CPC
- Leads
- Cost per lead
- Qualified leads
- Cost per qualified lead
- Applications
- Approvals
- Funded deals
- Cost per funded deal
- Lost lead reasons

## 14. Testing Warnings

A campaign may fail for reasons outside the ads.

Possible issues:

- Weak offer
- Unclear qualification rules
- Poor landing page
- Slow lead response
- High fees
- Complicated process
- Unapproved claims limiting copy strength
- Poor sales follow-up
- Platform restrictions
- Wrong target area

## 15. Starting Recommendation

Launch one controlled Google Search campaign first.

Review it carefully for two to four weeks before expanding into heavier social, LinkedIn, or broader awareness campaigns.
