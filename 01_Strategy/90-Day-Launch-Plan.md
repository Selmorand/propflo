# PropFlo 90-Day Launch Plan

## Purpose

This document defines the first 90 days of the PropFlo marketing launch.

The goal is to move carefully from planning to launch, then from launch to optimisation.

## 1. Main Principle

The first 90 days should be treated as a controlled launch.

Do not try to do everything at once.

The first priority is to prove which channels generate qualified leads that can become funded deals.

## 2. 90-Day Summary

### Month 1

Foundation, compliance, landing pages, tracking, lead handling, and campaign preparation.

### Month 2

Controlled campaign launch, early optimisation, SEO content, and partner outreach.

### Month 3

Improve lead quality, scale working channels, reduce wasted spend, and prepare the next-stage growth plan.

## 3. Month 1: Foundation

### Goal

Prepare the marketing system before spending money on ads.

### Key Tasks

- Complete discovery meeting
- Complete compliance questions
- Confirm approved claims
- Confirm restricted claims
- Confirm required disclaimers
- Confirm product terms
- Confirm qualification criteria
- Confirm lead handling process
- Confirm access and assets
- Finalise seller landing page brief
- Finalise form plan
- Finalise FAQ brief
- Prepare tracking plan
- Prepare Google Ads campaign structure
- Prepare keyword and negative keyword lists
- Prepare first ad copy drafts
- Prepare partner outreach framework

### Required Client Input

PropFlo must provide:

- Legal entity details
- Finance provider details
- Product details
- Qualification rules
- Fees and repayment information
- Disclaimers
- Privacy policy
- Terms and conditions
- Brand assets
- Platform access
- Lead handling contacts

### Month 1 Output

By the end of Month 1, the project should have:

- Approved claims
- Approved disclaimers
- Approved landing page structure
- Approved form structure
- Tracking plan
- Campaign plan
- Lead handling process
- Launch checklist

## 4. Month 2: Controlled Launch

### Goal

Launch with controlled spend and gather real data.

### Key Tasks

- Build or finalise seller landing page
- Build or finalise qualification form
- Build or finalise thank-you page
- Build or finalise FAQ page
- Install and test tracking
- Set up Google Ads campaign
- Load keywords
- Load negative keywords
- Load approved ad copy
- Test conversions
- Launch Google Search Ads
- Review search terms regularly
- Review lead quality weekly
- Add negative keywords
- Pause poor traffic
- Improve landing page wording if needed
- Start first SEO content item
- Start small partner outreach list

### Month 2 Output

By the end of Month 2, the project should have:

- Live Google Search campaign
- Working tracking
- Lead source data
- First lead quality feedback
- First optimisation log entries
- Initial SEO content started
- Initial partner outreach started

## 5. Month 3: Optimisation And Scaling

### Goal

Improve what works and reduce what does not.

### Key Tasks

- Review cost per lead
- Review cost per qualified lead
- Review applications
- Review approvals
- Review funded deals, if available
- Improve keyword targeting
- Expand successful keyword groups
- Reduce poor-performing ad groups
- Improve landing page FAQs
- Improve form questions
- Publish more SEO content
- Continue partner outreach
- Prepare monthly report
- Prepare 90-day review
- Recommend next-stage budget

### Month 3 Output

By the end of Month 3, the project should have:

- Campaign performance data
- Lead quality data
- Cost per qualified lead estimate
- Early cost per funded deal estimate, if available
- Improved landing page
- Improved negative keyword list
- Better understanding of sales bottlenecks
- Recommendation to scale, adjust, or restructure

## 6. Weekly Rhythm During Launch

Each week, review:

- Ad spend
- Search terms
- Leads
- Qualified leads
- Not-qualified reasons
- Sales feedback
- Tracking issues
- Landing page issues
- Budget pacing
- Optimisation actions

## 7. Monthly Rhythm

At the end of each month, prepare:

- Monthly report
- Optimisation summary
- Lead quality review
- Budget recommendation
- Next-month action plan

## 8. Success Criteria

The first 90 days should be judged by:

- Quality of leads
- Cost per qualified lead
- Applications started
- Approvals
- Funded deals
- Lead response time
- Sales feedback quality
- Tracking accuracy
- Campaign learning

Do not judge success by impressions, likes, or clicks alone.

## 9. Warning Signs

Watch for:

- Many personal loan enquiries
- Many leads without property sale status
- Slow lead response
- Missing sales feedback
- High spend with no qualified leads
- Unclear qualification rules
- Disapproved ads
- Broken tracking
- Landing page confusion

## 10. 90-Day Review Questions

At the end of 90 days, answer:

1. Did Google Search Ads produce qualified leads?
2. What was the cost per qualified lead?
3. Did any leads become funded deals?
4. Which keywords worked?
5. Which searches wasted money?
6. Did the landing page explain the offer clearly?
7. Did PropFlo respond quickly enough?
8. Did partner outreach show potential?
9. Should ad spend increase?
10. Should the next phase focus on ads, SEO, partners, or automation?

## 11. Starting Recommendation

Use the first 90 days to build evidence.

Only scale once PropFlo can clearly see which channels produce qualified leads and funded deals.
