# PropFlo Marketing Strategy

## Purpose

This document is the working strategy for PropFlo marketing.

It should guide the proposal, website structure, advertising, content, tracking, reporting, and monthly campaign management.

## 1. Business Summary

PropFlo offers bridging finance to property sellers.

The core purpose of the marketing is to help qualifying property sellers access funds linked to a property sale before the transfer process is complete.

## 2. Main Business Problem

Property sellers often wait for the transfer process to complete before they receive their sale proceeds.

During this waiting period, they may need funds for deposits, moving costs, rates clearance, bond shortfalls, repairs, living expenses, business cash flow, or other urgent commitments.

## 3. Core Marketing Message

Property sold but still waiting for transfer?

PropFlo helps qualifying sellers access funds linked to their property sale before transfer is complete.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo before use.

## 4. Primary Audiences

### Property Sellers

People who have sold or are selling a property and need access to funds before transfer.

### Estate Agents

Agents who work with sellers and may identify clients who need bridging finance.

### Conveyancing Attorneys

Transfer attorneys who understand where sellers are in the transfer process and may help confirm transaction status.

### Bond Originators

Property finance professionals who may know sellers or buyers facing timing-related finance issues.

### Property Developers and Investors

People involved in property transactions where cash flow timing may be important.

## 5. Buyer Triggers

A seller may need PropFlo when:

- A property has been sold but transfer has not completed.
- They need money before proceeds are paid out.
- They need to settle costs linked to the move or transaction.
- They are waiting for attorneys, banks, or transfer registration.
- They have a time-sensitive cash-flow problem.

## 6. Trust Barriers

PropFlo marketing must answer these concerns clearly:

- Is this legitimate?
- Is this safe?
- What does it cost?
- Who qualifies?
- How quickly can funds be paid?
- What documents are required?
- Who repays the finance?
- What happens if transfer is delayed?
- What happens if the sale falls through?
- Is my personal information protected?

## 7. Channel Strategy

### Google Search Ads

Primary direct lead channel.

This captures people already searching for bridging finance, property seller finance, or access to sale proceeds before transfer.

### SEO Content

Long-term trust and organic visibility channel.

This helps explain the product, answer common questions, and support Google Ads landing pages.

### Estate Agent Referral Programme

High-value partner channel.

Agents are close to sellers and may identify the need before the seller searches online.

### Conveyancer Referral Programme

High-trust partner channel.

Conveyancers are close to the transaction process, but messaging must be careful and professional.

### Meta Retargeting

Support channel.

Used to re-engage people who visited the website but did not enquire.

### LinkedIn

Partner and credibility channel.

Used for estate agency principals, conveyancers, bond originators, and property professionals.

### Email and WhatsApp Follow-Up

Conversion support channel.

Used to guide leads through the next steps after enquiry, subject to consent and POPIA requirements.

## 8. First 90-Day Plan

### Month 1: Foundation

- Confirm product and compliance details.
- Create approved claims and disclaimers.
- Build landing page structure.
- Set up tracking.
- Prepare Google Ads campaigns.
- Prepare basic SEO content plan.
- Prepare referral partner messaging.
- Prepare reporting dashboard.

### Month 2: Controlled Launch

- Launch Google Search Ads with controlled budget.
- Launch retargeting if compliant.
- Publish initial educational content.
- Start partner outreach.
- Review lead quality weekly.
- Improve landing pages based on questions and objections.

### Month 3: Optimise and Scale

- Cut poor keywords.
- Improve best-performing campaigns.
- Increase budget only where lead quality is strong.
- Expand content based on real enquiries.
- Improve partner process.
- Report on qualified leads, applications, approvals, and funded deals.

## 9. Success Metrics

The campaign should not be judged by likes or clicks only.

Main metrics:

- Website enquiries
- Phone calls
- WhatsApp enquiries
- Qualified leads
- Applications started
- Applications approved
- Deals funded
- Cost per qualified lead
- Cost per funded deal
- Lead response time
- Referral partner leads

## 10. Initial Recommendation

Start with a controlled 90-day launch.

Do not spend heavily until tracking, landing pages, lead handling, and compliance wording are correct.

The first goal is not volume.

The first goal is to prove which channel produces qualified leads that can become funded deals.
