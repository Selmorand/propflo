# PropFlo Channel Plan

## Purpose

This document explains the recommended marketing channels for PropFlo and the role each channel should play.

The channel plan prevents random marketing activity. Every channel must have a clear job.

## 1. Channel Strategy Summary

PropFlo should not rely on one channel only.

The recommended channel mix is:

- Google Search Ads for direct high-intent leads
- SEO content for long-term visibility and trust
- Estate agent referrals for high-trust partner leads
- Conveyancer referrals for professional credibility
- Meta retargeting for follow-up awareness
- LinkedIn for partner marketing
- Email and WhatsApp for lead nurturing
- Reporting and CRM tracking for performance management

## 2. Google Search Ads

### Role

Primary direct lead generation channel.

### Why It Matters

Google Search captures people who are already looking for bridging finance, property seller finance, or help accessing sale proceeds before transfer.

These people usually have stronger intent than people seeing a random social media advert.

### Example Search Themes

- property bridging finance
- bridging finance for property sellers
- property seller finance
- access money before property transfer
- advance on property sale proceeds
- sold house waiting for transfer money
- seller advance finance
- finance before property transfer

### Risks

- Financial services advertising restrictions
- Expensive clicks
- Low-quality searches if keywords are too broad
- Platform verification delays
- Compliance-related ad disapprovals

### Starting Recommendation

Start with tightly controlled exact and phrase match keywords.

Avoid broad match at the beginning.

Review search terms regularly and add negative keywords quickly.

## 3. SEO Content

### Role

Long-term trust, organic traffic, and educational support.

### Why It Matters

People researching financial products need clear information before they trust a provider.

SEO content also supports Google Ads by improving landing page relevance and answering common questions.

### Recommended Topics

- What is property bridging finance?
- How does bridging finance work for property sellers?
- Can I access money before property transfer?
- What documents are needed for property bridging finance?
- How long does property transfer take?
- What happens if property transfer is delayed?
- Bridging finance for estate agents to understand
- Bridging finance and conveyancing explained simply

### Risks

- SEO takes time
- Content must be accurate
- Legal and financial claims must be approved
- Thin or generic content will not build trust

### Starting Recommendation

Publish practical educational content first.

Avoid hype.

Answer real seller questions clearly.

## 4. Estate Agent Referral Programme

### Role

High-trust partner lead generation.

### Why It Matters

Estate agents are close to sellers and often know when a seller has a cash-flow problem before the seller searches online.

A referral system can become more valuable than paid ads if managed properly.

### Recommended Materials

- Estate agent partner landing page
- One-page PDF explainer
- WhatsApp referral script
- Email introduction template
- Referral process guide
- FAQ for agents
- LinkedIn outreach message

### Risks

- Agents need clear instructions
- Referral rules must be approved
- Commission or referral fees must be legally checked
- Poor partner communication can damage trust

### Starting Recommendation

Start with a small list of known estate agents.

Do not launch a large partner programme until the process is tested.

## 5. Conveyancer Referral Programme

### Role

Professional trust and transaction-stage lead generation.

### Why It Matters

Conveyancers are close to the property transfer process and understand when proceeds may be delayed.

However, this channel must be handled carefully because attorneys may be cautious about recommending finance products.

### Recommended Materials

- Conveyancer information page
- Professional introduction email
- Process explanation
- Compliance-approved wording
- Document checklist
- Clear referral boundaries

### Risks

- Conservative audience
- Legal caution
- Slower relationship building
- Need for professional credibility

### Starting Recommendation

Use a respectful education-first approach.

Do not push aggressive sales language.

## 6. Meta Retargeting

### Role

Support channel for people who already visited the website.

### Why It Matters

Some visitors will not enquire on the first visit.

Retargeting can remind them of the solution and bring them back when they are ready.

### Recommended Use

- Retarget website visitors
- Promote simple explainer content
- Promote FAQ content
- Promote qualification check page
- Promote callback option

### Risks

- Financial product ad restrictions
- Lower lead intent than Google Search
- Poor-quality leads if targeting is too broad
- Sensitive wording can lead to ad rejection

### Starting Recommendation

Do not use Meta as the main lead source at first.

Use it mainly for retargeting and education.

## 7. LinkedIn

### Role

Partner marketing and credibility building.

### Why It Matters

LinkedIn is useful for reaching estate agency owners, property professionals, conveyancers, developers, and business decision-makers.

It is probably better for B2B partner development than direct seller leads.

### Recommended Use

- Founder or company posts
- Partner education posts
- Estate agent outreach
- Conveyancer outreach
- Property industry credibility
- Case-style educational content

### Risks

- Slow direct lead generation
- Expensive paid ads
- Needs professional content
- Requires consistent relationship-building

### Starting Recommendation

Begin with organic LinkedIn and direct outreach before heavy paid LinkedIn advertising.

## 8. Email Follow-Up

### Role

Lead nurturing and conversion support.

### Why It Matters

Many leads need explanation before they continue.

Email can send useful information, document checklists, next steps, and reminders.

### Recommended Use

- Lead enquiry confirmation
- Document checklist
- Explanation of next steps
- FAQ follow-up
- Partner onboarding emails
- Monthly partner updates

### Risks

- POPIA consent requirements
- Poor list quality
- Too many emails can reduce trust
- Unclear opt-out process can create problems

### Starting Recommendation

Keep the first email sequence short and useful.

Do not over-automate before the sales process is understood.

## 9. WhatsApp Follow-Up

### Role

Fast response and human support.

### Why It Matters

For urgent finance enquiries, WhatsApp may be more effective than email.

Many South African users prefer quick WhatsApp communication.

### Recommended Use

- Callback confirmation
- Document checklist
- Sales handover
- Application follow-up
- Partner referrals

### Risks

- Consent and opt-out requirements
- Manual handling load
- Poor message control
- Sensitive personal information

### Starting Recommendation

Use WhatsApp carefully.

Start with simple approved scripts.

Do not collect sensitive financial information casually over WhatsApp unless the client confirms the process is acceptable.

## 10. Channel Priority

### Phase 1 Priority

- Google Search Ads
- Seller landing page
- Tracking and reporting
- SEO FAQ content
- Estate agent referral structure

### Phase 2 Priority

- Meta retargeting
- Partner outreach
- LinkedIn credibility posts
- Email follow-up
- WhatsApp scripts

### Phase 3 Priority

- SEO content expansion
- Referral partner programme
- Conversion rate optimisation
- Budget scaling
- Advanced automation

## 11. What Not To Prioritise First

Do not prioritise these at the beginning:

- TikTok
- Large social media posting volume
- Expensive LinkedIn ads
- Broad awareness campaigns
- Generic brand videos
- Influencer marketing
- Mass email campaigns
- Complicated automation before the lead process is proven

## 12. Main Principle

Each channel must have a job.

If a channel does not produce useful traffic, qualified leads, partner relationships, or measurable trust, it should not receive major budget.
