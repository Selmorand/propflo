# PropFlo Offer Structure

## Purpose

This document defines how the PropFlo offer should be structured for marketing, landing pages, adverts, partner material, and proposal planning.

The goal is to make the offer clear before public campaigns are created.

## 1. Main Principle

A good marketing campaign cannot fix an unclear offer.

Before campaigns launch, PropFlo must clearly define:

- Who the offer is for
- Who the offer is not for
- What problem it solves
- What the next step is
- What the client may qualify for
- What approval depends on
- What fees and repayment terms apply
- What claims may be used publicly

## 2. Core Offer Summary

### Draft Offer

PropFlo helps qualifying property sellers explore bridging finance linked to their property sale before transfer is complete.

### Approval Status

Not yet approved.

### Approved Version

To be confirmed by PropFlo.

## 3. Primary Problem

Property sellers may have sold a property but still need to wait for the transfer process to complete before receiving sale proceeds.

During this waiting period, they may need funds for:

- Moving costs
- Rates or levies
- Repairs
- Bond or settlement-related costs
- Deposit on another property
- Living expenses
- Business cash flow
- Other time-sensitive commitments

Final problem wording must be approved by PropFlo.

## 4. Primary Audience

The main offer is for:

- Property sellers
- Sellers with a property sale in progress
- Sellers waiting for transfer
- Sellers who may need access to funds before proceeds are paid
- Sellers who may meet PropFlo's qualification criteria

Final audience definition must be approved by PropFlo.

## 5. Excluded Audience

PropFlo must confirm who should not be targeted.

Possible exclusions:

- People who have not sold a property
- People without a signed Offer to Purchase
- People without a transferring attorney
- People looking for personal loans
- People looking for payday loans
- People looking for debt consolidation
- People outside supported areas
- People whose property transaction cannot be verified

Final exclusion criteria must be approved by PropFlo.

## 6. Qualification Requirements

PropFlo must confirm whether the following are required:

- Signed Offer to Purchase
- Transferring attorney appointed
- Property sale in progress
- Seller identity verification
- Property value confirmation
- Bond settlement information
- Sale proceeds verification
- Minimum advance amount
- Maximum advance amount
- Supported property type
- Supported geographic area

## 7. Value Proposition

### Draft Value Proposition

PropFlo gives qualifying property sellers a clear way to explore funding linked to their property sale while they wait for transfer to complete.

### Simpler Version

Property sold but still waiting for transfer?

PropFlo can help qualifying sellers check whether sale-linked bridging finance may be an option.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo.

## 8. Main CTA

Recommended primary CTA:

Check if you qualify

Reason:

It is clear, soft, and avoids overpromising.

## 9. Secondary CTA

Recommended secondary CTAs:

- Speak to PropFlo
- Request a callback
- Learn how it works
- Start an enquiry

## 10. Offer Proof Points

PropFlo must confirm which proof points may be used.

Possible proof points:

- Years in operation
- Number of sellers assisted
- Funding processed
- Professional partnerships
- Speed of response
- Simple process
- Secure document handling
- Local South African market experience
- Registered or compliant operating status, if applicable

Do not invent proof points.

## 11. Trust Elements Needed

The offer should be supported by:

- Clear process explanation
- Clear qualification wording
- Clear disclaimer
- Privacy policy
- Terms and conditions
- Human contact option
- Phone number
- Email address
- WhatsApp contact
- Legal entity information
- Professional partner information

## 12. Offer Risks

The offer may perform poorly if:

- Fees are unclear
- Qualification criteria are unclear
- Approval process is slow
- Lead response is slow
- Website copy overpromises
- Ads attract personal loan seekers
- Disclaimers are missing
- PropFlo cannot provide sales feedback
- The offer is not competitive

## 13. Offer Testing Angles

Test these angles carefully:

### Angle 1: Transfer Gap

Property sold but still waiting for transfer?

### Angle 2: Qualification Check

Check whether you may qualify for sale-linked bridging finance.

### Angle 3: Process Clarity

A simple enquiry process for qualifying property sellers.

### Angle 4: Partner Support

Help sellers understand their options while waiting for transfer.

## 14. Required Client Answers

Before finalising the offer, PropFlo must answer:

1. What exact product is being offered?
2. Who legally provides the finance?
3. Who qualifies?
4. Who does not qualify?
5. What fees apply?
6. How is repayment handled?
7. What timing claims may be used?
8. What disclaimers are required?
9. What proof points may be used?
10. What wording is not allowed?

## 15. Starting Recommendation

Do not write final ads until this offer structure is approved.

The offer must be clear before budget is spent.
