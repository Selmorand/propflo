# PropFlo Sales Handover

## Purpose

This document defines how marketing leads should be handed over to PropFlo's sales or consultant team.

The goal is to make sure leads are followed up quickly, tracked properly, and reported back to Interon for campaign optimisation.

## 1. Main Principle

Marketing creates the opportunity.

Sales converts the opportunity.

If PropFlo does not handle leads quickly and consistently, campaign performance will suffer even if the adverts and landing pages are working.

## 2. Lead Handover Point

A lead is handed over to PropFlo when one of the following happens:

- A website form is submitted
- A qualification form is submitted
- A WhatsApp enquiry is received
- A phone call is generated
- A partner referral is submitted
- An email enquiry is received

## 3. Required Lead Notification

Each new lead should notify the responsible PropFlo person or team.

Notification should include:

- Name
- Cell number
- Email
- Preferred contact method
- Lead source
- Campaign name, if available
- Landing page
- Property location
- Sale status
- Amount required, if provided
- Date and time received

## 4. Recommended Lead Owner

PropFlo should assign one person as the primary lead owner.

This person is responsible for:

- Checking new leads
- Assigning leads if needed
- Making sure leads are contacted
- Updating lead status
- Recording qualification outcome
- Reporting funded deals back to Interon

## 5. Lead Response Standard

Recommended response standard:

- Contact within 15 minutes where possible
- Contact within 1 hour as a minimum during working hours
- Same-day response for all working-hour leads
- Next working morning response for after-hours leads

## 6. Sales Team Responsibilities

PropFlo must:

- Contact leads quickly
- Ask qualification questions
- Record lead stage
- Record qualification result
- Request documents where needed
- Update application status
- Record declined, lost, or funded outcomes
- Share lead quality feedback with Interon

## 7. Interon Responsibilities

Interon will:

- Generate and optimise marketing campaigns
- Improve lead source quality
- Review campaign performance
- Review lead quality feedback
- Adjust targeting and messaging where needed
- Report on campaign performance
- Recommend improvements

Interon is not responsible for closing leads unless separately agreed.

## 8. Required Feedback From PropFlo

For each lead, PropFlo should record:

- Was the lead contacted?
- Was the lead qualified?
- If not qualified, why?
- Did the lead submit documents?
- Was an application submitted?
- Was the application approved?
- Was the deal funded?
- If lost, why was it lost?

## 9. Weekly Feedback Loop

During the first 90 days, Interon and PropFlo should review leads weekly.

Questions to review:

1. Are leads being contacted quickly enough?
2. Are the leads relevant?
3. Which campaigns are producing poor leads?
4. Which campaigns are producing good leads?
5. Are people confused by the offer?
6. Are people asking the same questions repeatedly?
7. Are qualification rules clear enough?
8. Is the form asking the right questions?
9. Are any ads attracting the wrong audience?
10. Should budget be moved?

## 10. Monthly Sales Feedback

At the end of each month, PropFlo should provide:

- Total leads received
- Total qualified leads
- Total applications
- Total approvals
- Total funded deals
- Total declined leads
- Total lost leads
- Reasons for lost leads
- Lead quality rating by channel

## 11. Lead Quality Rating

Use this simple rating system:

### A-Grade Lead

Strong fit. Seller appears qualified and ready to proceed.

### B-Grade Lead

Possible fit. Needs more information or documents.

### C-Grade Lead

Weak fit. May qualify later but not urgent or unclear.

### D-Grade Lead

Poor fit. Unlikely to qualify.

### Invalid Lead

Spam, duplicate, irrelevant, or outside service scope.

## 12. Campaign Improvement Notes

Interon needs sales feedback to improve campaigns.

Examples:

- If many leads have no Offer to Purchase, ads should clarify the requirement.
- If many leads need too little funding, forms should ask amount required earlier.
- If many leads are outside the service area, location targeting should be tightened.
- If many leads ask about fees, landing pages should explain costs more clearly.
- If many leads are worried about legitimacy, trust sections should be improved.

## 13. Starting Recommendation

Before launch, PropFlo must confirm:

- Who receives leads
- Who contacts leads
- Expected response time
- Lead tracking method
- Weekly feedback process
- Monthly reporting process

Campaigns should not launch until this handover process is agreed.
