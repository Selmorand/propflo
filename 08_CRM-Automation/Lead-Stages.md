# PropFlo Lead Stages

## Purpose

This document defines the lead stages that should be used in the CRM, spreadsheet, or lead tracking system.

The goal is to make sure every lead is tracked consistently from first enquiry to final outcome.

## 1. Main Principle

A lead is not successful just because someone filled in a form.

The important question is:

Did the lead become a qualified enquiry, application, approval, or funded deal?

## 2. Recommended Lead Stages

### 1. New Lead

The lead has just been received.

No one has contacted the person yet.

### 2. Contact Attempted

PropFlo has tried to contact the lead by phone, WhatsApp, or email.

The lead has not yet responded.

### 3. Contacted

PropFlo has successfully spoken to or received a reply from the lead.

### 4. Not Qualified

The lead does not meet the minimum requirements.

Possible reasons:

- Property not sold
- No signed Offer to Purchase
- No transferring attorney
- Property type not accepted
- Area not supported
- Required amount too low or too high
- Transaction not suitable
- Duplicate enquiry
- Spam or irrelevant enquiry

### 5. Qualified

The lead appears to meet the basic requirements and can move to the next step.

### 6. Documents Requested

PropFlo has asked the lead for supporting documents.

### 7. Documents Received

The lead has submitted the required documents.

### 8. Application Submitted

The application has been submitted for review or approval.

### 9. Approved

The application has been approved.

### 10. Declined

The application has been declined.

### 11. Offer Sent

PropFlo has sent the finance offer or terms to the client.

### 12. Offer Accepted

The client has accepted the finance offer.

### 13. Funded

The deal has been funded.

This is the most important conversion stage.

### 14. Lost

The lead was valid but did not become a funded deal.

Possible reasons:

- Client changed mind
- Client used another provider
- Transfer completed before funding
- Client did not submit documents
- Client did not accept terms
- Could not contact client
- Process took too long

### 15. Duplicate

The same lead was already captured.

### 16. Spam

The enquiry is not a real opportunity.

## 3. Required Lead Fields

Every lead should ideally include:

- Lead ID
- Date received
- Name
- Surname
- Cell number
- Email
- Preferred contact method
- Source
- Medium
- Campaign
- Landing page
- Lead type
- Property location
- Property sale status
- Offer to Purchase status
- Transferring attorney status
- Estimated sale value
- Estimated amount required
- Current lead stage
- Qualification reason
- Next follow-up date
- Sales notes
- Final outcome

## 4. Lead Type Options

Use these lead types:

- Property seller
- Estate agent
- Conveyancer
- Bond originator
- Developer
- Investor
- General enquiry
- Unknown

## 5. Qualification Notes

When a lead is marked as not qualified, the reason should be recorded.

This helps improve the marketing.

If many leads are not qualified for the same reason, the landing page, form, ads, or targeting may need to be changed.

## 6. Lost Lead Notes

When a lead is marked as lost, the reason should be recorded.

This helps identify whether the problem is:

- Marketing
- Sales response time
- Product terms
- Pricing
- Approval criteria
- Timing
- Competitor offer
- Client trust

## 7. Reporting Notes

For monthly reporting, group lead stages into these categories:

### Enquiries

- New Lead
- Contact Attempted
- Contacted

### Qualified Opportunities

- Qualified
- Documents Requested
- Documents Received
- Application Submitted

### Sales Outcomes

- Approved
- Declined
- Offer Sent
- Offer Accepted
- Funded
- Lost

### Exclusions

- Duplicate
- Spam

## 8. Starting Recommendation

Use this lead stage structure before campaigns go live.

If lead stages are not tracked consistently from the start, it will be difficult to know which marketing channels are producing real business value.
