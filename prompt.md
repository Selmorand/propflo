## Core compliance findings to incorporate

### 1. Google Ads

Google may classify the product under its personal-loan advertising policy.

If that classification applies, important requirements may include:

- repayment in full must not be required in less than 61 days;
- minimum and maximum repayment periods must be disclosed;
- maximum APR or equivalent locally calculated rate may need to be disclosed;
- a representative example showing total cost and fees may be required;
- physical business address and associated fees should be clearly available on the destination page;
- advertiser and business verification may apply.

Strategic implication:

Google Search remains a potentially high-value channel because of strong search intent, but the strategy must not guarantee Google Ads eligibility until the actual finance agreement and contractual periods have been reviewed.

Use wording such as:

"Google Search advertising will be activated subject to product eligibility, platform policy compliance and approval."

Do not state that Google Ads definitely cannot be used.

### 2. Facebook and Instagram

Meta allows lending and financial-service advertising subject to restrictions.

Incorporate the following strategy:

- advertise only to appropriate adult audiences;
- use the Financial Products and Services Special Ad Category where required;
- expect audience-targeting restrictions;
- avoid misleading or aggressive financial claims;
- avoid collecting unnecessary sensitive financial information directly through advertising lead forms;
- use Meta primarily for awareness, education, retargeting and initial enquiries;
- direct sensitive application information into a secure application process.

Recommended funnel:

Ad
→ Educational landing page
→ Eligibility enquiry
→ Consent-based follow-up
→ Secure application process

### 3. LinkedIn

LinkedIn should remain primarily a B2B partner and relationship channel for:

- estate agency principals;
- estate agents;
- conveyancing firms;
- property developers;
- bond originators;
- relevant property-sector partners.

Financial product ads may require:

- privacy policy;
- accurate contact information;
- legally required disclosures;
- repayment and cost information where applicable;
- adult targeting.

Do not position LinkedIn as the primary direct consumer acquisition channel.

Do not remove TikTok completely from future channel planning.

### 6. Financial advertising claims

Create a structured claims-control system.

It must contain:

#### Approved claims

Examples:

- Access eligible proceeds before transfer is completed.
- Check whether you qualify.
- Speak to a PropFlo consultant.
- Manage the cash-flow gap during the property transfer process.

Final approval must still depend on actual product processes.

#### Conditional claims

Examples:

- funds within a specific number of hours;
- up to a specific percentage of proceeds;
- specific approval periods;
- specific fee or pricing claims;
- statements about speed.

These may only be used where evidence and operational processes support them.

#### Prohibited or high-risk claims

Examples:

- guaranteed approval;
- guaranteed cash;
- no credit checks;
- everybody qualifies;
- pre-approved finance;
- cheapest bridging finance;
- blacklisted applicants welcome;
- free credit.

Create a practical approval system rather than merely listing these in the strategy.

### 7. POPIA and direct marketing

The strategy must treat electronic direct marketing as a controlled consent process.

Recommended journey:

Marketing campaign or referral
→ Customer enquiry
→ Recorded consent
→ Approved follow-up channel
→ Secure application process

The system requirements should include:

- consent wording;
- consent record;
- consent source;
- consent date and time;
- communication channel permission;
- unsubscribe handling;
- opt-out handling;
- suppression list;
- source control for imported contacts;
- management of third-party lists;
- WhatsApp consent records;
- privacy-policy links.

Do not recommend buying consumer email, SMS or WhatsApp databases.

### 8. WhatsApp

Position WhatsApp primarily as a consent-based communication and nurturing channel.

Suitable uses include:

- answering questions;
- process explanation;
- document checklist communication;
- appointment scheduling;
- application reminders;
- progress updates.

Sensitive information should move into an approved secure application or document-upload process.

Avoid recommending ordinary WhatsApp conversations as the main collection point for:

- identity documents;
- financial records;
- full banking information;
- property transaction documentation.

### 9. Referral partner compliance

The referral programme must distinguish between:

#### Introduction only

The partner provides PropFlo's contact information or educational material.

#### Consent-based referral

The customer explicitly agrees that their information can be provided to PropFlo.

#### Application assistance or intermediary involvement

The partner actively explains, assists with or participates in the finance application process.

The strategy must state that referral remuneration, commissions and active intermediary involvement require legal review before a large-scale programme is launched.

Do not state that all referral arrangements are prohibited.

Do not assume that estate agents may automatically be paid referral commission.

The partner strategy must include a legal approval checkpoint before commission structures are implemented.
