# PropFlo Website Form Plan

## Purpose

This document defines the recommended lead capture form structure for PropFlo.

The goal is to collect enough information to qualify the enquiry without making the form too long, intrusive, or risky.

## 1. Main Form Principle

The first form should not try to collect everything.

It should capture enough information for PropFlo to decide whether the person may be a suitable lead and whether a consultant should contact them.

Sensitive documents and detailed financial information should only be requested through PropFlo's approved process.

## 2. Primary Form Name

Check If You Qualify

## 3. Primary Form Purpose

Capture enquiries from property sellers who may need bridging finance linked to a property sale.

## 4. Recommended Form Fields

### Personal Details

- First name
- Surname
- Cell number
- Email address
- Preferred contact method

### Property Sale Details

- Property location
- Has the property been sold?
- Has an Offer to Purchase been signed?
- Has a transferring attorney been appointed?
- Is there an existing bond on the property?

### Finance Need

- Estimated property sale value
- Estimated amount required
- Reason for needing funds
- How soon do you need assistance?

### Consent

- Consent to be contacted
- Privacy policy acknowledgement
- POPIA consent wording

## 7. Suggested Consent Wording

I agree that PropFlo may contact me about my enquiry. I understand that assistance is subject to qualification, verification, approval, and PropFlo's terms and conditions.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo.

## 8. Suggested Privacy Wording

By submitting this form, you agree that your information may be processed for the purpose of responding to your enquiry.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo and linked to the privacy policy.

## 9. Recommended Thank-You Message

Thank you. Your enquiry has been received.

A PropFlo consultant will review your information and contact you to discuss the next step.

Assistance is subject to qualification, verification, approval, and PropFlo's terms and conditions.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo.

## 10. Fields To Avoid On The First Form

Avoid asking for these too early unless PropFlo specifically requires them:

- ID number
- Bank account details
- Full financial history
- Full property documents
- Bond account number
- Attorney client documents
- Buyer information
- Sensitive supporting documents

These can be requested later through the approved process.

## 11. Lead Notification

When the form is submitted, PropFlo should receive an alert with:

- Name
- Contact details
- Preferred contact method
- Property location
- Property sale status
- Offer to Purchase status
- Transferring attorney status
- Amount required
- Lead source
- Campaign data if available

## 12. Tracking Requirements

The form should trigger:

- GA4 event
- Google Ads conversion
- Lead source capture
- Thank-you page visit
- CRM or lead tracker entry

## 13. Starting Recommendation

Use one clear form first.

Do not create multiple complicated forms until the lead process has been tested.
