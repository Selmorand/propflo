# PropFlo Seller Landing Page Brief

## Purpose

This document defines the content and layout brief for the main seller-focused landing page.

This page is intended for property sellers who may need bridging finance before property transfer is complete.

## 1. Page Goal

Convert suitable property sellers into qualified enquiries.

The page should help visitors understand:

- What PropFlo does
- Who it may help
- When it may apply
- What the next step is
- That approval is subject to qualification and verification

## 2. Target Audience

Primary audience:

Property sellers who have sold or are selling a property and may need access to funds before transfer is complete.

Possible situations:

- Property sold but transfer is not complete
- Waiting for sale proceeds
- Need funds for moving costs
- Need funds for rates, levies, repairs, or settlement-related costs
- Need short-term cash flow while waiting for transfer

## 3. Main Page Message

Suggested draft:

Property sold but still waiting for transfer?

PropFlo helps qualifying sellers explore funding linked to their property sale before transfer is complete.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo.

## 4. Hero Section

### Headline

Property sold but still waiting for transfer?

### Supporting Text

PropFlo helps qualifying property sellers explore bridging finance linked to their property sale before transfer is complete.

### Primary CTA

Check if you qualify

### Secondary CTA

Speak to a consultant

### Disclaimer Line

Subject to qualification, verification, approval, and PropFlo's terms and conditions.

Final disclaimer must be approved by PropFlo.

## 5. Problem Section

### Heading

The sale may be agreed, but the money may not be available yet

### Content Notes

Explain that a seller may accept an offer, but the transfer process can still take time before sale proceeds are paid.

Keep this simple. Do not make legal promises about the transfer process.

## 6. How PropFlo Helps

### Heading

How PropFlo may help

### Content Notes

Explain that PropFlo reviews the seller's situation and checks whether bridging finance linked to the property sale may be possible.

Avoid promising approval or payout timing unless approved.

## 7. Who This Is For

### Heading

Who this may be for

Suggested bullets:

- Sellers with a property sale in progress
- Sellers waiting for transfer to complete
- Sellers with a signed Offer to Purchase
- Sellers with a transferring attorney appointed
- Sellers who need funds before final proceeds are paid

Final qualification details must be confirmed by PropFlo.

## 8. How It Works

### Heading

A simple process

Suggested steps:

1. Submit an enquiry.
2. PropFlo reviews your basic details.
3. A consultant contacts you.
4. Required documents may be requested.
5. Your transaction is verified.
6. If approved, PropFlo explains the next step.

Final process wording must be confirmed by PropFlo.

## 9. Documents Section

### Heading

What you may need

Possible documents:

- Signed Offer to Purchase
- Seller identity document
- Property details
- Transferring attorney details
- Bond or settlement information, if applicable

Final document requirements must be approved by PropFlo.

## 10. Trust Section

### Heading

Clear information before you proceed

Content should explain:

- No approval is guaranteed
- Qualification and verification are required
- Terms and conditions apply
- Personal information must be handled responsibly
- A consultant will explain the next step

## 11. FAQ Section

Include these questions:

1. What is property bridging finance?
2. Who may qualify?
3. Do I need a signed Offer to Purchase?
4. Do I need a transferring attorney?
5. What documents may be needed?
6. How long does approval take?
7. What does it cost?
8. How is repayment handled?
9. What happens if transfer is delayed?
10. Is my information protected?

Answers must be approved by PropFlo.

## 12. Final CTA Section

### Heading

Check whether PropFlo may be able to assist

### CTA

Check if you qualify

### Supporting Text

Submit a short enquiry and a PropFlo consultant can explain the next step.

## 13. Form Placement

Recommended form positions:

- Main CTA button in hero
- CTA after process section
- CTA after FAQ section

Do not place a long form above the fold unless the page is very simple and mobile-friendly.

## 14. Design Notes

The page should feel:

- Professional
- Calm
- Clear
- Trustworthy
- Simple
- Property-focused

Avoid:

- Aggressive loan styling
- Cash imagery
- Fake urgency
- Overpromising
- Too much legal jargon
- Long dense paragraphs

- Thank-you page visit
