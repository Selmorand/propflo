# PropFlo Conveyancer Partner Page Brief

## Purpose

This document defines the content and layout brief for the conveyancer partner landing page.

The goal is to provide clear, professional information for conveyancers, transfer attorneys, and property transfer professionals.

## 1. Page Goal

Help conveyancers understand PropFlo without pressuring them to sell or promote the service.

The page should explain:

- What PropFlo does
- When the service may be relevant
- What information may be needed
- How the process respects professional boundaries
- How PropFlo communicates with sellers
- What conveyancers should not be asked to promise

## 2. Target Audience

Primary audience:

- Conveyancing attorneys
- Transfer attorneys
- Conveyancing secretaries
- Property transfer departments

Secondary audience:

- Property law firms
- Estate agency legal support teams
- Bond cancellation support teams

## 3. Main Page Message

Suggested draft:

Clear information for property transfer professionals.

PropFlo helps qualifying property sellers explore bridging finance linked to their property sale before transfer is complete.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo.

## 4. Hero Section

### Headline

Information for conveyancers and property transfer professionals

### Supporting Text

PropFlo works with qualifying property sellers who may need access to funds linked to a property sale before transfer is complete.

This page explains the process, documentation considerations, and communication boundaries.

### Primary CTA

Request information

### Secondary CTA

Speak to PropFlo

### Disclaimer Line

Subject to qualification, verification, approval, and PropFlo's terms and conditions.

Final disclaimer must be approved by PropFlo.

## 5. Professional Context Section

### Heading

Where PropFlo may fit in the transfer process

### Content Notes

Explain that sellers may experience a funding gap while waiting for transfer to complete.

Keep wording factual.

Do not suggest that the conveyancer is responsible for recommending, approving, or arranging finance unless PropFlo has confirmed the process.

## 6. How PropFlo Works With Sellers

### Heading

How PropFlo handles seller enquiries

Suggested steps:

1. Seller submits an enquiry.
2. PropFlo checks basic qualification.
3. PropFlo requests required information.
4. Property sale details may need to be verified.
5. PropFlo explains the next step to the seller.
6. Any assistance remains subject to qualification, verification, approval, and terms.

Final process wording must be confirmed by PropFlo.

## 7. Information That May Be Needed

### Heading

Information that may be requested

Possible information:

- Confirmation that the property sale is in progress
- Signed Offer to Purchase
- Transferring attorney details
- Property details
- Bond or settlement-related information, if applicable
- Expected transfer timing, if available

Final document and information requirements must be approved by PropFlo.

## 8. Professional Boundaries

### Heading

Clear professional boundaries

Content should explain:

- Conveyancers should not promise approval.
- Conveyancers should not confirm payout timing unless authorised.
- Conveyancers should not provide financial advice on behalf of PropFlo.
- Sellers should deal directly with PropFlo regarding the finance enquiry.
- Any information sharing must follow the correct consent and privacy process.

## 9. What Conveyancers May Say

### Heading

What conveyancers may say

Suggested wording:

PropFlo may be able to assist qualifying sellers who need access to funds linked to their property sale before transfer is complete.

You can contact PropFlo directly so their team can explain the process and check whether you may qualify.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo.

## 10. What Conveyancers Should Not Say

### Heading

What conveyancers should not say

Avoid:

- You are guaranteed to qualify.
- Funds will be paid today.
- There are no costs.
- No verification is required.
- The transfer will not be affected.
- The transaction cannot fail.
- Approval is automatic.
- The attorney will arrange everything.

## 11. FAQ Section

### Heading

Common questions

Recommended questions:

1. What does PropFlo do?
2. Which sellers may use PropFlo?
3. Does the seller need a signed Offer to Purchase?
4. What information may PropFlo request?
5. Does PropFlo require transaction verification?
6. What should conveyancers avoid promising?
7. Does PropFlo deal directly with the seller?
8. What consent is needed before information is shared?
9. What happens if a seller does not qualify?
10. Who should conveyancers contact for more information?

## 13. Design Notes

The page should feel:

- Professional
- Formal
- Restrained
- Clear
- Trustworthy
- Process-focused

Avoid:

- Sales pressure
- Commission-led messaging
- Emotional seller language
- Over-promising
- Legal advice claims
- Aggressive finance copy
