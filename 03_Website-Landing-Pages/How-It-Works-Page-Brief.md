# PropFlo How It Works Page Brief

## Purpose

This document defines the content and layout brief for the PropFlo "How It Works" page.

The goal is to explain the process clearly so sellers, estate agents, conveyancers, and other property professionals understand the basic journey before making an enquiry.

## 1. Page Goal

Explain the PropFlo process in plain language.

The page should help visitors understand:

- What happens after an enquiry
- What information may be needed
- Why verification is required
- That approval is not guaranteed
- What the next step is
- Who handles the process

## 2. Target Audience

Primary audience:

- Property sellers

Secondary audience:

- Estate agents
- Conveyancers
- Bond originators
- Property professionals
- Referral partners

## 3. Main Page Message

Suggested draft:

PropFlo follows a clear process to help qualifying property sellers explore bridging finance linked to a property sale before transfer is complete.

Final wording must be approved by PropFlo.

## 4. Hero Section

### Headline

How PropFlo works

### Supporting Text

A simple process for qualifying property sellers who may need access to funds linked to their property sale before transfer is complete.

### Primary CTA

Check if you qualify

### Secondary CTA

Speak to PropFlo

### Disclaimer Line

Subject to qualification, verification, approval, and PropFlo's terms and conditions.

Final disclaimer must be approved by PropFlo.

## 5. Process Overview Section

### Heading

A clear process from enquiry to decision

### Content Notes

Explain that PropFlo first reviews basic information before requesting more details or documents.

Keep the wording simple.

Do not promise approval, payout timing, or funding unless approved.

## 6. Step 1: Submit An Enquiry

### Heading

Step 1: Submit an enquiry

### Content Notes

The seller completes a short enquiry form or contacts PropFlo.

The purpose is to capture basic details about the seller, property sale, and funding need.

## 7. Step 2: Basic Qualification Check

### Heading

Step 2: Basic qualification check

### Content Notes

PropFlo checks whether the enquiry appears to meet the basic requirements.

This may include property sale status, signed Offer to Purchase status, transferring attorney status, property location, and estimated funding need.

Final qualification criteria must be approved by PropFlo.

## 8. Step 3: Consultant Contact

### Heading

Step 3: A consultant contacts you

### Content Notes

A PropFlo consultant contacts the seller to confirm details and explain the next step.

The consultant may ask basic questions about the sale, transfer process, and required amount.

## 9. Step 4: Documents May Be Requested

### Heading

Step 4: Documents may be requested

### Content Notes

If the enquiry appears suitable, PropFlo may request supporting documents.

Possible documents may include:

- Signed Offer to Purchase
- Seller identity document
- Property details
- Transferring attorney details
- Bond or settlement information, if applicable

Final document requirements must be approved by PropFlo.

## 10. Step 5: Verification

### Heading

Step 5: Information is verified

### Content Notes

PropFlo may need to verify information linked to the property sale and transfer process.

This helps confirm whether the enquiry can continue.

Do not describe legal or financial verification steps unless PropFlo approves the wording.

## 11. Step 6: Decision And Next Step

### Heading

Step 6: PropFlo explains the outcome

### Content Notes

If the application can continue, PropFlo explains the next step.

If the application does not qualify, the seller is informed.

Do not promise approval or payout.

## 12. Step 7: Funding If Approved

### Heading

Step 7: Funding if approved

### Content Notes

If approved and accepted, funding may be arranged according to PropFlo's process and terms.

Final funding and payout wording must be approved by PropFlo.

## 13. Important Notes Section

### Heading

Important things to know

Suggested points:

- Not every enquiry will qualify.
- Verification is required.
- Approval is not guaranteed.
- Terms and conditions apply.
- Fees and repayment details must be confirmed by PropFlo.
- Personal information must be handled through the approved process.

## 16. Design Notes

The page should feel:

- Simple
- Step-by-step
- Trustworthy
- Calm
- Process-focused
- Easy to scan

Use:

- Numbered steps
- Simple icons
- Short paragraphs
- Clear CTA buttons
- FAQ section
- Disclaimer line

Avoid:

- Long legal explanations
- Aggressive finance language
- Cash imagery
- Guaranteed approval wording
- Confusing process detail
