# PropFlo Marketing Proposal Draft

## Purpose

This document is the working draft for the PropFlo marketing proposal.

It is structured for client review. It will be finalised once PropFlo confirms product details, compliance wording, approved claims, budget, and start date.

## 1. Proposal Title

PropFlo 90-Day Marketing Launch Proposal

## 2. Prepared For

PropFlo

## 3. Prepared By

Interon

## 4. Proposal Summary

PropFlo requires a measurable marketing system that generates enquiries from property sellers, qualifies them, tracks them through to funded deals, and improves month by month.

Interon proposes a controlled 90-day launch covering strategy, compliance preparation, landing page planning, tracking, paid media setup, content, referral partner structure, and monthly optimisation.

The purpose of the first 90 days is not to spend heavily.

The purpose is to prove which channels produce qualified leads that become funded deals, and to build a repeatable system that can be scaled with confidence.

## 5. Business Opportunity

Property sellers often experience a funding gap between accepting an offer and receiving the sale proceeds after transfer.

During this waiting period, sellers may need funds for moving costs, rates or levy clearance, bond shortfalls, deposits on a new property, business cash flow, or other time-sensitive commitments.

PropFlo can help qualifying sellers access funds linked to the property sale before transfer is complete.

Final wording, qualification criteria, and disclaimers must be approved by PropFlo before public use.

## 6. Marketing Funnel and Measurement Frame

PropFlo marketing is measured across the full seller journey, not on clicks alone.

The funnel is:

Traffic → Lead → Qualified Lead → Application → Approval → Funded Deal

The most important business metric is cost per funded deal, where sales data is available.

Interon will track and report:

- Website sessions and lead source
- Total leads
- Qualified leads
- Applications
- Approvals
- Funded deals
- Cost per lead
- Cost per qualified lead
- Cost per funded deal
- Lead response time
- Best and worst performing channel

This is only possible if PropFlo updates lead status regularly. Advertising platforms alone cannot show real business results.

## 7. Strategic Approach

The recommended approach is a controlled 90-day launch, not a large opening spend.

The reasons:

- Financial services advertising is regulated and platform-restricted. Wording must be tested and approved.
- Cost per click for bridging finance keywords is high. Broad targeting wastes money quickly.
- The value of a funded deal is high. A single well-qualified lead can justify significant setup cost, so lead quality matters more than lead volume.
- Sales response time drives conversion. The launch must be paced so PropFlo can respond to every enquiry.
- Real search terms, real objections, and real lead quality only appear after live campaigns run. The system must be able to learn and improve, not just launch.

Interon's role is to build the system, launch it in controlled steps, measure what happens, and recommend where to scale.

## 8. Recommended Channels and Priority

PropFlo should not rely on one channel.

The recommended channel priority is:

1. Google Search Ads — primary direct lead channel, high intent
2. Seller landing page and conversion funnel — the destination that turns clicks into enquiries
3. Tracking and lead source attribution — required before spending money
4. SEO and educational content — long-term visibility and trust
5. Estate agent partner marketing — high-trust referral channel
6. Conveyancer information and referral relationships — professional credibility
7. Meta retargeting — re-engage website visitors
8. LinkedIn partner marketing — B2B credibility and outreach
9. Email and WhatsApp follow-up — nurture and speed up conversion
10. CRM and automation improvements — reduce lost leads over time

Each channel has a job. Channels that do not produce useful traffic, qualified leads, or partner relationships will not receive major budget.

## 9. Audience Approach

PropFlo has one primary audience and four secondary audiences. They will not receive the same messaging.

| Audience | Priority | Language | Best channel | Primary CTA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Estate agents | 1 | Practical partner language | LinkedIn, partner page, email, PDF | Request partner information |
| Property sellers | 2 | Simple, reassuring | Google Search, seller landing page, FAQ, WhatsApp | Check if you qualify |
| Conveyancers | 3 | Formal, careful, education-first | LinkedIn, professional email, information page | Request information |
| Bond originators | 4 | Professional relationship | LinkedIn, direct outreach, partner PDF | Request partner information |
| Developers and investors | 5 | Commercial | LinkedIn, direct outreach, referrals | Speak to PropFlo |

Sellers are the primary lead source. Partner audiences are addressed once the seller journey is proven.

## 10. Recommended 90-Day Plan

### Month 1: Foundation

- Confirm product and compliance details
- Confirm approved claims and disclaimers
- Finalise strategy
- Plan and build landing pages
- Set up tracking and lead source capture
- Prepare Google Ads and Meta retargeting campaigns
- Prepare content and partner framework
- Confirm lead handling and response process

### Month 2: Controlled Launch

- Launch Google Search Ads with controlled budget
- Launch retargeting if compliance-approved
- Start SEO content publishing
- Start estate agent outreach
- Review lead quality weekly
- Refine landing page wording based on real questions
- Add negative keywords, pause poor-performing search terms

### Month 3: Optimise and Decide

- Refine campaigns and expand what works
- Improve landing pages and lead handling
- Review estate agent and conveyancer response
- Report on cost per lead, cost per qualified lead, cost per funded deal
- Prepare a scale, adjust, or restructure recommendation for month 4 onward

## 12. Measurement and Reporting

Interon will provide:

- Weekly optimisation notes during launch (search terms, lead quality, spend pacing)
- Monthly report covering spend, leads, qualified leads, applications, approvals, funded deals, cost per stage, channel performance, wasted spend, and recommendations
- Quarterly review covering channel effectiveness, conversion bottlenecks, and a scale, adjust, or restructure recommendation

Reports link marketing activity to sales outcomes. Clicks, impressions, and reach are not treated as final business results.

## 15. What PropFlo Must Provide

For the project to start and run correctly, PropFlo must provide:

**Business and compliance:**

- Legal entity details and finance provider status
- Product terms, fees, and repayment structure
- Qualification criteria and required documents
- Approved disclaimers, privacy policy, and terms
- Approval of all public-facing claims

**Operational:**

- Lead owner and notification method
- Response time target and process
- Weekly lead quality feedback during launch
- Regular updates on lead status through to funded or lost

Delays in providing this information will delay campaign launch.

## 16. Approval Requirements Before Launch

PropFlo must review and approve, before any campaign goes live:

- Core offer wording
- Qualification wording
- Disclaimer wording
- Landing page copy
- Form and consent wording
- Google Ads copy
- Email and WhatsApp templates
- Partner outreach wording

Interon does not provide legal, financial, or regulatory compliance approval.

## 17. Key Limitations

Marketing can generate awareness, enquiries, and leads. It cannot guarantee:

- A specific number of qualified leads, applications, approvals, or funded deals
- Platform approval of every advert (Google, Meta, and LinkedIn restrict financial services advertising)
- Specific search rankings or conversion rates
- Results if the offer, response time, or lead handling process is weak

Campaign performance depends on product clarity, compliance approval, lead response time, offer competitiveness, landing page quality, and tracking accuracy.

The full assumptions and limitations document is provided separately and should be signed with the proposal.
