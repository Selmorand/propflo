# PropFlo Client Onboarding Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist controls the process from proposal to implementation.

It makes sure PropFlo and Interon are aligned before strategy, advertising, landing pages, tracking, and monthly management begin.

## Stage 1: Internal Preparation

Before meeting with PropFlo, Interon should prepare:

- Project folder created
- Compliance questions prepared
- Strategy document started
- Scope of work drafted
- Budget proposal drafted
- Assumptions and limitations drafted
- Initial channel recommendation prepared
- Initial 90-day launch structure prepared

## Stage 2: Discovery Meeting

Purpose:

Understand the business, finance product, client goals, sales process, compliance limits, and budget expectations.

Questions to cover:

- What is PropFlo's main growth goal?
- Who is the ideal customer?
- Which property sellers qualify?
- Which property sellers do not qualify?
- What is the average funded deal value?
- What is the average revenue per funded deal?
- What cost per funded deal would be acceptable?
- What channels have already been tested?
- What sales process is currently used?
- Who follows up with leads?
- How quickly can leads be contacted?
- What geographic areas are targeted?
- What marketing assets already exist?

Output:

- Discovery notes
- Missing information list
- Updated strategy direction

## Stage 3: Compliance and Claims Review

Purpose:

Confirm what may and may not be said publicly.

Required from PropFlo:

- Legal entity name
- Finance provider details
- Registration or licence details, if applicable
- Approved product description
- Approved fee wording
- Approved repayment wording
- Approved qualification wording
- Required disclaimers
- Privacy policy
- Terms and conditions
- Platform verification documents, if needed

Output:

- Approved claims list
- Restricted claims list
- Disclaimer list
- Sign-off record

Important:

No ads, landing pages, emails, or WhatsApp scripts should go live before this stage is completed.

## Stage 4: Proposal Approval

Purpose:

Confirm the commercial structure and scope.

Proposal should include:

- Recommended 90-day launch plan
- Once-off setup fee
- Landing page or website cost
- Monthly management fee
- Suggested ad spend
- Third-party costs
- Client responsibilities
- Limitations
- Minimum term
- Review point

Output:

- Signed proposal
- Approved budget
- Confirmed start date

## Stage 5: Access and Assets

Purpose:

Collect everything needed to build and launch the marketing system.

Required access or assets may include:

- Website CMS access
- Domain or hosting access, if needed
- Google Analytics access
- Google Tag Manager access
- Google Ads account access
- Meta Business Manager access
- LinkedIn Page access
- Google Business Profile access, if used
- Brand logo
- Brand colours
- Fonts
- Existing brochures
- Existing forms
- Existing client emails
- Existing privacy policy
- Existing terms and conditions
- Sales team contact details

Output:

- Access checklist completed
- Asset folder completed

## Stage 6: Website and Landing Page Build

Purpose:

Create pages that convert traffic into qualified enquiries.

Core pages:

- Property seller landing page
- Estate agent partner page
- Conveyancer partner page
- FAQ page
- Thank-you page
- Contact or qualification form

Output:

- Draft pages
- Client review
- Compliance review
- Final approved pages

## Stage 7: Tracking Setup

Purpose:

Make sure campaign performance can be measured.

Tracking items:

- Google Analytics 4
- Google Tag Manager
- Google Ads conversion tracking
- Form submission tracking
- Phone click tracking
- WhatsApp click tracking
- Thank-you page tracking
- UTM structure
- Lead source tracking sheet or CRM fields

Output:

- Tracking test completed
- Test leads recorded
- Conversion events confirmed

## Stage 8: Campaign Setup

Purpose:

Prepare campaigns before launch.

Campaign setup includes:

- Google Search Ads campaigns
- Keyword groups
- Negative keywords
- Ad copy
- Campaign budgets
- Location targeting
- Conversion actions
- Meta retargeting campaign, if approved
- LinkedIn partner campaign, if approved

Output:

- Campaigns built
- Internal review completed
- Client approval received

## Stage 9: Pre-Launch Testing

Purpose:

Catch problems before spending money.

Test:

- Landing page links
- Forms
- Thank-you pages
- Email notifications
- WhatsApp links
- Phone links
- Tracking events
- UTM tracking
- Mobile display
- Page speed
- Disclaimer visibility
- Privacy and consent wording

Output:

- Launch checklist approved

## Stage 10: Controlled Launch

Purpose:

Launch with controlled spend and monitor lead quality.

First launch period:

- Start with limited daily budget
- Check search terms daily at first
- Check lead quality
- Check tracking
- Check form notifications
- Check sales response time
- Pause poor keywords quickly
- Add negative keywords
- Adjust ad copy if needed

Output:

- Week 1 launch review
- Early optimisation actions

## Stage 11: Weekly Optimisation

Purpose:

Improve performance using real campaign data.

Weekly review:

- Spend
- Clicks
- Search terms
- Cost per lead
- Lead quality
- Qualified leads
- Applications started
- Sales feedback
- Landing page issues
- Budget pacing

Output:

- Weekly optimisation log
- Recommended changes

## Stage 12: Monthly Reporting

Purpose:

Show what happened, what it means, and what should happen next.

Monthly report should include:

- Spend by channel
- Leads by channel
- Qualified leads
- Applications
- Approvals
- Funded deals, if available
- Cost per lead
- Cost per qualified lead
- Cost per funded deal
- Best-performing campaigns
- Wasted spend
- Landing page insights
- Next-month actions

Output:

- Monthly report
- Next-month recommendations
- Budget recommendation

## Stage 13: 90-Day Review

Purpose:

Decide whether to scale, adjust, or restructure.

Review:

- Which channels worked?
- Which channels failed?
- What was the cost per qualified lead?
- What was the cost per funded deal?
- What was the sales conversion rate?
- Was the offer clear?
- Was lead response fast enough?
- Should ad spend increase?
- Should SEO/content increase?
- Should partner marketing increase?

Output:

- 90-day performance review
- Scaling recommendation
- Updated strategy
