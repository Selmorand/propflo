# PropFlo Assumptions and Limitations

## Purpose

This document defines the assumptions, limitations, and responsibilities that must be understood before PropFlo marketing work begins.

It protects both PropFlo and Interon by making clear what marketing can control, what the client must provide, and where legal, financial, sales, and platform limitations may apply.

## 1. Compliance Responsibility

Interon can assist with marketing strategy, campaign planning, content, landing pages, tracking, reporting, and campaign management.

Interon does not provide legal, financial, or regulatory compliance approval.

All legal, financial, credit, fee, repayment, approval, qualification, and timing claims must be reviewed and approved by PropFlo or its appointed legal/compliance adviser before publication.

## 2. Approved Claims

No public claims should be used until PropFlo confirms they are accurate and approved.

This includes claims such as:

- Fast approval
- Same-day approval
- Funds within 24 hours
- No upfront cost
- Guaranteed approval
- Access money before transfer
- Bridging finance
- Property seller finance
- Advance on sale proceeds

Any claim that may affect a seller's financial decision must be confirmed before use.

## 3. Platform Restrictions

Google, Meta, LinkedIn, and other advertising platforms may restrict or reject financial services advertising.

Campaigns may be delayed or limited if platform verification, policy review, business documentation, disclaimers, or account checks are required.

Interon cannot guarantee that every advert will be approved by an advertising platform.

## 4. Lead Quality

Marketing can generate awareness, traffic, enquiries, and leads.

Marketing cannot guarantee that every lead will:

- Qualify
- Submit documents
- Be approved
- Accept the offer
- Become a funded deal

Lead quality depends on the offer, targeting, landing page, compliance wording, response time, and the client sales process.

## 5. Sales Response Time

Campaign performance depends heavily on how quickly PropFlo responds to incoming leads.

Slow response times may reduce conversion rates.

PropFlo should ideally contact new leads as quickly as possible, especially if the enquiry comes through Google Ads, WhatsApp, or a callback form.

## 6. Offer Competitiveness

If the product fees, approval criteria, payout times, process, or terms are not competitive, marketing performance may be limited.

Marketing cannot fix a weak or unclear offer without changes to the product, process, or positioning.

## 7. Tracking Limits

Not every conversion can be tracked perfectly.

Tracking may be affected by:

- Browser privacy settings
- Cookie consent choices
- Ad blockers
- Offline phone calls
- WhatsApp conversations
- Manual follow-ups
- Incomplete CRM updates
- Cross-device behaviour

For accurate reporting, PropFlo must update lead status consistently.

## 8. SEO Timing

SEO and content marketing are long-term channels.

Organic visibility may take months to build.

Paid advertising can generate faster data, but SEO requires consistent publishing, technical quality, and time.

## 9. Ad Spend

Advertising spend is separate from Interon's setup or management fees.

Ad spend is paid by PropFlo directly to the advertising platforms unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Ad spend is not a guarantee of results.

## 10. Data Protection

PropFlo is responsible for ensuring that lead collection, storage, access, follow-up, and deletion processes comply with POPIA and any other applicable data protection requirements.

Interon can assist with practical setup, forms, consent wording placement, and workflow design, but legal responsibility must be confirmed by PropFlo.

## 11. Client Inputs Required

PropFlo must provide accurate information about:

- Legal entity details
- Finance provider details
- Registration or licence details
- Fees
- Repayment process
- Qualification criteria
- Required documents
- Approval process
- Payout process
- Disclaimers
- Privacy policy
- Terms and conditions
- Sales process
- Lead handling team

Delays in receiving this information may delay the campaign.

## 12. No Guaranteed Outcomes

Unless agreed separately in writing, Interon does not guarantee:

- A specific number of leads
- A specific number of applications
- A specific number of approvals
- A specific number of funded deals
- A specific cost per lead
- A specific return on ad spend
- Specific search rankings
- Platform approval of all adverts

The goal is to build, test, measure, and improve a controlled marketing system.

## 13. Recommended Starting Approach

The recommended approach is a controlled 90-day launch.

Month 1 should focus on foundation, compliance, tracking, landing pages, and campaign setup.

Months 2 and 3 should focus on launch, testing, optimisation, reporting, and controlled scaling.

Large ad spend should only be considered once tracking and lead quality are proven.
